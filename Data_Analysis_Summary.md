# Data Analysis & Scientific Validity Summary

This document provides a detailed breakdown of the statistical evidence supporting the efficacy of the ChatGPT-Assisted Problem-Based Learning (PBL) model.

## 📊 Comparative Performance Results
The study utilized a Quasi-Experimental Nonequivalent Control Group Design with the following results:

| Assessment Phase | Experimental Group (PBL + AI) | Control Group (Standard PBL) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| **Pre-test Mean** | 51.90 | 54.20 |
| **Post-test Mean** | 87.50 | 81.30 |
| **N-Gain Score** | **65.3% (Moderate)** | 38.7% (Low) |

## 🧪 Statistical Evidence
To ensure the reliability and significance of these findings, several statistical tests were conducted:

*   **T-Test Result**: An Independent Samples T-test confirmed a statistically significant difference in performance between the groups with a **p-value < 0.05**.
*   **Mastery Achievement**: **86.1%** of students in the AI-assisted group reached the mastery criterion, compared to only 55.6% in the control group[cite: 1].
*   **Instrument Reliability**: The assessment tools were verified with a high reliability coefficient of **0.842 (KR-20)**, ensuring consistent and stable results[cite: 1].

## 📈 Analysis & Discussion
The data indicates that integrating ChatGPT as a cognitive scaffolding tool within the PBL framework drives a significantly higher improvement in learning outcomes (65.3% N-Gain)[cite: 1]. This success is attributed to the "Human-in-the-Loop" architecture which mandates critical evaluation and cross-referencing of AI-generated data[cite: 1].
