# Transforming Digital Literacy through ChatGPT-Assisted Problem-Based Learning

## Project Overview
This repository contains the instructional design framework and research data for a study on integrating ChatGPT into the **Problem-Based Learning (PBL)** model. The project focuses on enhancing digital literacy and learning outcomes for 11th-grade students in Biology (Excretory System).

**Lead Researcher/Instructional Designer:** Iftinan R Azzah S

---

## 🚀 Key Impact (Evidence-Based)
*   **N-Gain Score:** **65.3% improvement** in the experimental group (Moderate Category) vs. 38.7% in the control group.
*   **Mastery Achievement:** **86.1%** of students reached mastery compared to 55.6% in the control group.
*   **Scientific Validity:** Statistically significant results with **p < 0.05** and an instrument reliability of **0.842 (KR-20)**.

---

## 🛠 The ADDIE Process
1.  **Analysis:** Identified gaps in 21st-century digital literacy skills.
2.  **Design:** Architected a **"Human-in-the-Loop"** framework where AI acts as a research assistant, not an answer machine.
3.  **Development:** Created a Biology module (Excretory System) integrated with ethical AI usage guidelines.
4.  **Implementation:** Applied a Quasi-Experimental Nonequivalent Control Group Design.
5.  **Evaluation:** Conducted pre-test and post-test assessments to quantify growth in critical thinking and content mastery.

---

## 🧠 Instructional Framework: Human-in-the-Loop
The module follows a 5-phase interaction cycle to prevent AI dependency:
*   **Phase 1 (Orientation):** Students use AI for cognitive scaffolding (analogies and context).
*   **Phase 2 (Investigation):** AI serves as a research assistant for molecular biological processes.
*   **Phase 3 (Validation - CRITICAL):** Students **must** cross-reference AI claims with peer-reviewed sources or textbooks.
*   **Phase 4 (Development):** AI acts as a brainstorming partner for creative slogans and PSA metaphors.
*   **Phase 5 (Evaluation):** Students submit a **"Prompt Log"** to reflect on AI hallucinations and biases.
