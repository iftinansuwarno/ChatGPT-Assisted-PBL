# Instructional Architecture: Human-in-the-Loop AI Integration

This project is built upon a **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)** architecture, ensuring that Artificial Intelligence serves as a cognitive scaffold rather than a replacement for critical thinking.

## 🏗️ The Framework: PBL + AI Scaffolding
The architecture synchronizes the 5 stages of Problem-Based Learning with targeted AI interactions:

1.  **Cognitive Scaffolding (Orientation)**: AI is used to lower the barrier to entry by providing analogies and simplifying complex jargon.
2.  **Research Augmentation (Investigation)**: AI acts as a high-speed research assistant to explain molecular and physiological mechanisms.
3.  **Mandatory Validation (The Critical Step)**: A structural requirement where students must verify AI technical claims against peer-reviewed sources (e.g., Biology Textbooks or Journals)[cite: 1].
4.  **Creative Synthesis (Development)**: AI functions as a brainstorming partner for designing health campaigns and public service announcements[cite: 1].
5.  **Metacognitive Reflection (Evaluation)**: Students analyze AI limitations, identifying "hallucinations" and biases in their Prompt Logs[cite: 1].

## 🧪 Pedagogical Logic
*   **Constructive Alignment**: Every AI interaction is mapped directly to Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs) and Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)[cite: 1].
*   **Bloom’s Taxonomy**: The design forces students to move beyond *Remembering* and *Understanding* into the realms of *Analyzing*, *Evaluating*, and *Creating*[cite: 1].
*   **Prompt Engineering for LXD**: The library utilizes role-playing and constraint-based prompting to ensure deep inquiry[cite: 1].

## 🛡️ Risk Mitigation
To prevent "AI Dependency," this architecture implements:
*   **Prompt Logs**: Documenting the evolution of student-AI dialogue[cite: 1].
*   **Human-Centric Validation**: No AI output is considered "final" without human verification against primary sources[cite: 1].
